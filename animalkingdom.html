Animal Kingdom

Disney’s Animal Kingdom, the largest theme park at Walt Disney World Resort, opened on April 22, 1998—Earth Day—as a celebration of nature, wildlife, and conservation. Guided by Walt Disney’s passion for animals and storytelling, the park blends the magic of Disney with the beauty of the natural world. It was designed to highlight the interconnectedness of all living things, bringing guests closer to nature while promoting environmental stewardship. Animal Kingdom is divided into themed lands: Oasis, Discovery Island, Africa, Asia, DinoLand U.S.A., and Pandora – The World of Avatar.

Oasis greets guests with lush greenery and exhibits of exotic animals. Discovery Island, the park’s hub, features the iconic Tree of Life, a stunning sculpture home to It’s Tough to Be a Bug, a 3D experience inspired by A Bug’s Life.

Africa is designed as the fictional village of Harambe, celebrating African culture and wildlife. Its centerpiece attraction, Kilimanjaro Safaris, offers an open-air journey through savannas, spotting animals like elephants, lions, and giraffes. Guests can also enjoy Festival of the Lion King, a vibrant stage show.

Asia transports visitors to the fictional kingdom of Anandapur, featuring lush landscapes and thrilling adventures. Highlights include Expedition Everest, a high-speed roller coaster through the Himalayas, and Kali River Rapids, a white-water rafting experience. Guests can also explore the Maharajah Jungle Trek to encounter tigers, komodo dragons, and more.

DinoLand U.S.A. offers prehistoric fun with Dinosaur, a thrilling journey to save a dinosaur from extinction, and carnival-style attractions.

Pandora immerses guests in the alien world of Avatar. Attractions include Avatar Flight of Passage, a breathtaking flight simulation, and Na’vi River Journey, a serene, bioluminescent boat ride.
