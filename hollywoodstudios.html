Hollywood Studios

Disney’s Hollywood Studios, located at Walt Disney World Resort, opened on May 1, 1989, as Disney-MGM Studios. Originally conceived as a working film and television production studio, the park was inspired by Hollywood’s Golden Age and Walt Disney’s love of entertainment. Over the years, it has evolved into a park that immerses guests in the magic of movies, television, and storytelling.

Hollywood Boulevard and Sunset Boulevard are areas capture the glamour of old Hollywood with retro architecture and lively entertainment. Hollywood Boulevard leads to the iconic Chinese Theatre, home to Mickey & Minnie’s Runaway Railway, a zany animated adventure. Sunset Boulevard features the thrilling Twilight Zone Tower of Terror and the high-speed coaster Rock ‘n’ Roller Coaster Starring Aerosmith.

Echo Lake hosts attractions like Indiana Jones Epic Stunt Spectacular, which showcases movie-making magic, and Star Tours: The Adventures Continue, a motion-simulated journey through the Star Wars galaxy.

Toy Story Land is a playful land that shrinks guests to toy-size for attractions like Slinky Dog Dash, a family-friendly roller coaster, and Toy Story Mania, an interactive 4D shooting game.

Galaxy’s Edge immerses guests in the Star Wars universe with attractions like Millennium Falcon: Smugglers Run and the groundbreaking Star Wars: Rise of the Resistance, offering thrilling intergalactic adventures.

Animation Courtyard celebrates Disney’s animation legacy with Star Wars Launch Bay and character meet-and-greets. Grand Avenue features Muppet-themed fun at Muppet*Vision 3D. Disney’s Hollywood Studios brings the magic of storytelling to life, making it a must-visit for film and adventure enthusiasts alike.
